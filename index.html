Strategy Rules

Entry: Buy Draw at half-time when score is 0-0. Entry price estimated as pre-game Pinnacle draw probability × 1.40 (HT factor calibrated from 2025-26 Polymarket live data: 24.1¢ pre-game → 34.0¢ at 0-0 HT on average).

Exit: Hold to full-time. Payout = 100¢ if draw, 0¢ if not.

Universe: All games across 5 top European leagues where score is 0-0 at half-time and Pinnacle closing odds are available.

  • n ≈ 530 qualifying games per season (roughly 38% of all games end 0-0 at HT)
  • Historical draw rate from 0-0 HT: ~36.3% across all leagues/seasons
  • Pinnacle fair-value HT draw price ≈ 36.5¢ (implied from odds)

Bundesliga Signal p = 0.018

Why Bundesliga stands out: Over 992 games (13 seasons), the Bundesliga shows a statistically significant draw overperformance at 0-0 HT — actual draw rate 37.9% vs market implied 34.7%, EV = +9.1%, z = 2.09, p = 0.018.

The other 4 leagues show no meaningful edge (|z| < 1.0). This suggests Bundesliga-specific market inefficiency — the draw market may be systematically underpriced in German football. Possible explanations: lower liquidity, different playing style (higher press, more counterattack patterns leading to reset after HT), or bookmaker model bias.

Caveat: p = 0.018 is borderline significant. With 5 leagues tested simultaneously, the Bonferroni-corrected threshold would be p < 0.01. This is a promising signal, not a certainty. Requires live-market validation on Polymarket for confirmation.

Data source: football-data.co.uk — free historical CSVs with match results, HT scores (HTHG/HTAG), and Pinnacle closing odds (PSCH/PSCD/PSCA) going back to 2000. Fair-value method: Pinnacle implied draw probability normalized to remove margin: P(draw) = (1/D) / (1/H + 1/D + 1/A). HT price estimated by multiplying pre-game probability by 1.40 (the empirical HT factor from Polymarket 2025-26 live data). P&L units: cents-per-game (if you stake $100/game, multiply P&L by 1).
